What is the Gut Microbiome?

The gut microbiome is the community of trillions of microorganisms (mainly bacteria, but also fungi, viruses, and archaea) that live in your digestive tract, especially in the large intestine. These microbes aren’t just “living there” — they interact closely with your body, influencing your digestion, immunity, brain, and even mood.

What Is Immunotherapy?

Immunotherapy is a type of medical treatment that uses a person's own immune system to fight diseases — most commonly cancer, but also autoimmune disorders, allergies, and infections.It boosts, trains, or restores the immune system’s natural ability to recognize and destroy harmful cells — like cancer cells or pathogens.

Gut Microbiome & Immunotherapy

The gut microbiome plays a vital role in regulating the immune system, directly influencing how the body responds to immunotherapy. Certain beneficial gut bacteria enhance immune cell activity, improving treatment effectiveness, while an imbalanced microbiome can reduce therapy success. Maintaining a healthy gut may boost the body’s ability to fight diseases through immunotherapy.
