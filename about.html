Project Goal

To develop an explainable machine learning model that classifies patient suitability for cancer immunotherapy based on gut microbiome data, providing interpretable insights into microbiota–immune interactions.

Problem Statement

Cancer immunotherapy is not effective for every patient. The gut microbiome plays a crucial role in modulating immune responses, but the relationship between specific microbes and immunotherapy success remains unclear. This project uses explainable AI to identify which patients are likely to benefit, helping doctors make more informed treatment decisions.

Objectives

• Analyze gut microbiome and immune response data.
• Identify microbial species influencing therapy outcomes.
• Train explainable ML models to predict immunotherapy suitability.
• Provide transparent and interpretable results.
• Create a user-friendly interface for clinical interpretation.

Methodology

1. Data Collection – Gut microbiome and clinical datasets.
2. Preprocessing – Cleaning, normalization, and feature extraction.
3. Model Development – Random Forest, XGBoost, and SHAP analysis.
4. Evaluation – Accuracy, precision, recall, and ROC-AUC metrics.
5. Explainability – Visualization of microbial and immune factors affecting predictions.

Impact

This project contributes to precision medicine by helping clinicians understand how gut microbiota affects immunotherapy response. It supports personalized treatment decisions, reduces the risk of ineffective therapies, and provides interpretable AI insights for future medical research.
